DEAD AXLE DESIGN // ELEGANCE IN SIMPLICITY

The dead axle implementation allows wheels to rotate independently of the axle — eliminating torsional stress, reducing rotational inertia, and yielding a more reliable drivetrain. Fewer moving failure points means higher match-to-match consistency.

ITERATION_LOG // DESIGN_EVOLUTION

V1 — INITIAL CONCEPT SEPT 2025

Standard mecanum drivetrain with direct-drive axle. Thick turret profile caused interference with shooter assembly. Indexer and lift added unnecessary complexity.

OUTCOME: REDESIGN REQUIRED — TURRET TOO BULKY, RELIABILITY INSUFFICIENT
V2 — DEAD AXLE + BELT DRIVE NOV 2025

Transitioned to dead axle design. Replaced direct drive with belt-driven mecanum. Eliminated indexer and lift — shooter feeds directly from transfer. Turret profile reduced significantly.

OUTCOME: IMPROVED EFFICIENCY + REDUCED PART COUNT
V3 — CNC ALUMINIUM + LIMELIGHT INTEGRATION JAN 2026

CNC-machined aluminium replaces 3D-printed structural parts. Limelight added for vision-based turret alignment. Belly pan lowered for CoG optimisation. Pedro Pathing fully integrated.

OUTCOME: CURRENT BUILD — COMPETITION-READY

LESSONS_LEARNED

COMPLEXITY KILLS RELIABILITY

Removing the indexer and lift reduced failure modes significantly. Every removed mechanism is one fewer thing to break under match pressure.

SENSOR FEEDBACK ENABLES CONSISTENCY

Manual turret alignment was inconsistent match-to-match. Vision-based auto-alignment (Limelight) made shooting accuracy repeatable regardless of driver input.

CAD BEFORE CUTTING

All V3 components fully modelled in CAD before CNC machining. Eliminated costly material waste from V1/V2 prototype iterations.

STUDENT-LED SKILL ACQUISITION

Lathe and CNC training obtained from industry mentor Sebastian Seminara — applied directly to robot components within the same build cycle.

DESIGN_RATIONALE // WHY IT WORKS

BELTED MECANUM DRIVETRAIN

Superior traction vs roller mecanum. Belt dampens shock loads. Holonomic movement enables precise field positioning during AUTO and TELEOP.

SINGLE FLYWHEEL SHOOTER

Fewer moving parts than dual-flywheel. Velocity-controlled via software for range adaptation. Rapid spin-up time reduces cycle duration.

BEARING-MOUNTED TURRET ROTATION

Custom bearing mount gives smooth 360° rotation with minimal friction. Low-profile design eliminates interference with shooter and intake systems.
